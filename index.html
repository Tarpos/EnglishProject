Fun Facts About Doctors!

The lives of doctors are very tough. No doubt about that. But there are many interesting aspects about them as well! Let us explore some fun facts about them!

1. There are 10.41 lakh doctors currently practicing in India which is a doctor is to patient (D:P) ratio of 0.74:1000, for a population of 1.40 billion (140 crores) as of December 2021. The WHO recommendation is a minimum D:P. ratio of 1:1000. Qatar has the highest D:P.

2. According to the World Bank, India’s health care spending, public and out of pocket, totals about 3.5 percent of its gross domestic product, less than half of the global average.

3. Every year on July 1, India celebrates National Doctors Day in honour of Dr Bidhan Chandra Roy, a Bharat Ratna awardee, former chief minister of West Bengal and one of the most popular Congress leaders from eastern India.

4. The earliest written record that mentions the practice of medicine is Hammurabi’s Code from the 18th century BC.

5. Doctors did not wear white coats till 1800.

6. Anandibai Gopalrao Joshi was the first female Indian physician. She was also the first woman in India to complete her studies in western medicine from the United States.

7. Galileo Galilei invented the thermometer.

8. To prove that the bacteria H. pylori could cause stomach ulcers, Barry J. Marshall drank a culture of H. pylori, developed an ulcer, and successfully treated it with antibiotics and won a Nobel Prize for it in 2005.

9. Balamurali Krishna "Bala" Ambati is an Indian American ophthalmologist, educator, and researcher. On May 19, 1995, he entered the Guinness Book of World Records as the world's youngest doctor, at the age of 17 years, 294 days.

10. One of the oldest known medical textbooks is the Sushruta Samhita, written in Sanskrit in India.

11. Egyptians were the first to practice surgery.

12. In the United States of America, doctors leave sponges and other medical tools inside of their patients about 6,000 times a year.

13. Hippocrates (460–377 BC) is commonly called the "Father of Medicine" or the "Father of Western Medicine." He is thought to be one of the first physicians to treat disease as being a result of natural rather than supernatural causes.

Isn't it extremely amazing that there are so many interesting aspects about doctors and their lives!
